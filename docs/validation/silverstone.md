## Analysis

**Top speed and the grip envelope validate; lap time and corner speed do not.**
The calibrated preset reaches 345.9 km/h against a ~330 km/h trap (+4.8%), and
its 4.60 g lateral peak sits 0.9 g under the published ~5.5 g — both inside the
methodology tolerances. The powertrain/drag balance and the overall grip
magnitude are therefore in the right ballpark.

The lap time, however, is 36% *slower* than the published qualifying lap, and the
minimum corner speed (52.8 km/h) is far below the ~95 km/h seen at the Village
hairpin. Two effects dominate this gap:

1. **Centerline, not racing line.** The QSS solver evaluates the speed limit
   along the track centerline. A real qualifying lap uses the full track width to
   straighten corners, raising the effective radius and the cornering speed —
   most dramatically through Silverstone's high-speed sequences (Copse,
   Maggotts/Becketts, Stowe). The centerline through the tight, measured apexes
   produces a much lower minimum speed than a driven line.

2. **Conservative cornering grip.** The calibrated tire set peaks at 4.6 g, below
   the ~5.5 g a real car sustains. Cornering speed scales with √(grip·radius), so
   a grip deficit compounds with the centerline-radius deficit to inflate the lap
   time. The higher-grip *default* set narrows the lap-time gap (99.8 s, +16%) and
   matches the lateral-g envelope better (6.19 g), but only by also reaching an
   unphysical 429 km/h top speed — it over-grips and over-speeds simultaneously.
   No single fixed parameter set reproduces both top speed and corner pace,
   because the model lacks the speed-dependent aero map and per-corner setup that
   let a real car do both.

**Optimizer.** The N = 80 Hermite-Simpson solve returns 109.5 s but with a large
equality violation (3.75 × 10¹): on real measured geometry with sharp curvature
transitions it does not reach a dynamically consistent solution within 60
Gauss-Newton iterations from the QSS warm start. Its lap time should be read as
not-yet-converged rather than as a validated figure; mesh refinement and more
iterations would be needed before treating the optimizer output as a reference
here.

**Conclusion.** Apex-14 reproduces Silverstone's geometry (−0.07% length), top
speed (+4.8%), and grip envelope (−0.9 g) within tolerance, which validates the
track import, aerodynamics, and tire-grip magnitude. It does not reproduce
qualifying lap time or minimum corner speed, and the discrepancy is explained by
modelling choices that are documented and expected: a centerline rather than a
racing line, a flat-track assumption (no Silverstone elevation or camber), and a
single fixed, conservative grip set. These are the limitations listed in the
methodology, and closing them — a racing-line objective (the collocation
optimizer, once converged), elevation/camber, and load- and thermal-sensitive
grip — is the path to a tighter lap-time match.
