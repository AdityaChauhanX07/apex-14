## 2. Scope pulled forward from the dynamic OCP (the IP solver)

The bound-capable primal-dual interior-point solver ([`ip-solver.md`](ip-solver.md)) was
built here but is **shared infrastructure for the deferred dynamic OCP** — built once,
per `recon.md` §1.4 ("to avoid building bound-handling infrastructure twice"). What it
proved, beyond unblocking the envelope OCP:

- **The Gauss-Newton projected-Newton bound deadlock is resolved.** GN floors at
  `eq_violation ≈ 3.40e-1` (25–28 of ~349 vars pinned at a bound); the IP barrier reaches
  `1.287e-7`. Locked by `ip_resolves_gn_bound_deadlock`. This retires the deferral in
  `gn-solver-bound-deadlock.md` / `PHYSICS_CHANGE.md` (2026-07-04/05) — the oval/Silverstone
  optimize goldens can be unpaused as **new** fixtures when the dynamic OCP needs them.
- **Real circuits reach machine-tight feasibility** at coarse meshes — overturning the
  original "cannot converge on real circuits" ([`real-track-convergence.md`](real-track-convergence.md) §4).
- **The `N ≥ 44` conditioning wall is characterized, not hand-waved** (§B.2): the periodic
  first-difference collocation `JeqᵀJeq` is a graph-Laplacian whose condition number grows
  like `N²`; larger `N` → slower `eq` contraction → the `mu`-coupled `rho` ramp grows the
  penalty → `rho·JeqᵀJeq` out-runs the Jacobi-preconditioned CG → the primal freezes. A
  positive-feedback loop. This diagnosis **is the design input** for the dynamic-OCP
  solver's preconditioning/continuation work.

> **Amendment (dynamic-ocp reconnaissance): the Spa item is an ATTRIBUTION test, not a
> recovery one.** This close originally called the descent over-carry "the business case"
> for the dynamic OCP, implying that OCP would *recover* the measured descent speed. It
> cannot, and the reason is structural rather than a matter of fidelity: the dynamic OCP is
> specified with **no driver model** and a **minimum-time** objective, while the measured
> driver ran the descent at median throttle **0.71** with median grip utilization **0.45**
> — conserving, not limited. A minimum-time solver will go *faster* through that section,
> not slower, so "the dynamic OCP recovers the descent over-carry" is not an achievable
> acceptance criterion as worded. What the dynamic OCP can do is **bound and attribute** the
> residual: if min-time-with-transients is still at or above QSS through the descent, the
> residual is **confirmed driver behaviour** and `power_scale ≈ 0.80` is correctly read as
> an effective parameter absorbing driver conservatism (exonerating the correlation model);
> if it comes out meaningfully *slower*, then transient/load-transfer physics QSS ignores
> genuinely costs time there and part of the residual is model physics after all. Both
> outcomes are results. Full reasoning and the proposed test in
> [`../dynamic-ocp/recon.md`](../dynamic-ocp/recon.md) §6.3 (contradiction **C1** in §7.1).

## 4. Negative results — stated as results

These are findings, not gaps. Each was tested and rejected, and each saved later work:

- **Spa load-transfer null.** Swapping the point-mass friction circle for the load-sensitive
  envelope does **not** shrink the Spa descent over-carry — the descent is not grip-limited
  (median grip utilization 0.45, 8 % of stations near the limit), so no grip law can move it.
  The residual is longitudinal/transient. Calibration-free; survives the aero bridge.
  (`correlation_spa_envelope.md`.)
- **Curvature-joint explanation falsified.** The original "synthetic Silverstone fails
  because arc/straight curvature joints can't be resolved" is wrong on every clause: the
  spline-smoothed C2 **real** Silverstone converges no better, refining the mesh makes
  feasibility **worse**, and the collocation *rate* defect is already tiny (~6e-4). The
  cause was a mistuned AL schedule. (`real-track-convergence.md` §1–4.)
- **h-normalization rejected.** Row-scaling the equalities by `1/ds` to make the integrated
  defect mesh-invariant *starves* the AL penalty (~100× weaker) and blows up the rate defect
  — the equalities were never the problem. (`real-track-convergence.md` §3.)
- **Adaptive-`rho` prototype rejected.** An online productivity-gated penalty ceiling
  (`rho_grow_floor`) converged the oval and real circuits but could not bridge the
  circle↔real gap (the circle's large-but-stalling infeasibility is online-indistinguishable
  from real progress) and did not fix the `N ≥ 44` wall. Reverted; `rho_max` documented as a
  problem-scale knob instead. (`real-track-convergence.md` §B.4.)
- **`a_y`-as-control rejected.** Making `a_y` the lateral control (O(1) envelope constraint)
  **froze** the OCP: `∂xi'/∂a_y ≈ 1/v² ≈ 1/1600`, so the envelope-clamped control has no
  authority over the heading defect and equalities stall at `eq ≈ 2.7`. `kappa_cmd` gives
  `∂xi'/∂kappa_cmd ≈ 1` and converges. (`free-trajectory-ocp.md`, `math/envelope_ocp.md`.)
- **`a_y` boundary is linear in `g_z`, not `√g_z`.** The task brief anticipated `√g_z`; the
  accel-space friction-circle radius is `∝ μ·g_z` (first order). The honest linear test was
  written. (`trim-solver.md`.)
- **The CLI ran the un-tuned config (fixed).** `optimize --envelope` had silently used
  `recommended_ip_config`'s *old* defaults (`al=0.25, rho_max=3e4`), `MaxIter`-ing on
  Monza/Catalunya/Spielberg; the tuned schedule is now in `recommended_ip_config` itself.
  (`real-track-convergence.md` §B.5.)
- **Aero-blindness (found, then fixed).** The envelope originally derived downforce from the
  fixed `f1_default` `AeroModel`, invisible to `CarParams.lift_coeff` — the setup optimizer's
  #1 lever. The `scaled_for_car` bridge resolved it (`lift_coeff` sensitivity 0.4 → 6.2 s),
  but **weakened the rank gate to marginal** (§3) and shifted every calibrated-car lap ~1–2 s
  slower — the honest cost of correct aero. (`setup-envelope.md`.)
